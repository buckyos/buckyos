1. Introduction

Welcome to BuckyOS Desktop (the "Software").

The Software is a decentralized desktop software project initiated, maintained, or collaboratively organized by the BuckyOS open-source project (the "Project"). The Software is designed to provide users with a desktop runtime environment centered on decentralization, privacy protection, user autonomy, and a local-first approach.

This Agreement is intended to explain:

1. the open-source nature of the Software and the boundaries of applicable rights;
2. the basic freedoms users enjoy, and the basic responsibilities users assume, when using the Software;
3. the boundary between the Software and related online services, third-party services, and local operational services; and
4. risk disclosures, disclaimers, and limitations of liability associated with the use of the Software.

5. Decentralization Principles and Privacy Commitments

5.1 Decentralization Principles

The Software is designed around decentralization, user autonomy, and a local-first model. Under normal circumstances, its core design goals include:

- avoiding reliance on any single centralized party as a mandatory control point;
- processing data, to the extent possible, on user devices, in local environments, or through nodes managed by users themselves;
- minimizing reliance on centralized data aggregation architectures; and
- allowing users to decide whether to connect to external services, whether to share data, and the scope of any such sharing.

5.2 Privacy and Visibility Boundaries

When users rely only on the local functionalities of the Software or on decentralized environments under their own control, the Project does not, in principle, by default view, aggregate, centrally store, or analyze user content data.

However, users understand and agree that:

1. if a user voluntarily connects to Third-Party Services, Extension Services, or remote nodes, relevant data may be transmitted, processed, or stored according to the handling logic of those services;
2. if a user enables AI services, network forwarding, remote synchronization, cloud relays, domain resolution, or other online capabilities, relevant requests, metadata, or content may enter systems controlled by the corresponding service providers; and
3. certain logs, diagnostic data, crash information, request metadata, or security incident information may be collected or processed where the user has expressly authorized such collection, enabled relevant features, joined testing programs, or where collection is otherwise legally required.

5.3 User Control
